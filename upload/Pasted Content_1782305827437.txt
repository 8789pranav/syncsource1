Below is a **proper industry-level Leave Management module blueprint** for your HRMS, with **multi-tenant configuration, leave type creation, leave rule builder, workflow, dynamic forms, UI structure, fields, settings, employee application flow, approval flow, payroll/attendance integration, and admin controls**.

Enterprise HRMS leave systems are not just “apply leave and approve leave.” SAP SuccessFactors separates **Time Off / absence recording** from attendance and supports leave request workflows; Zoho People supports customizable leave policies, accrual, approval, location/role/department-based policies; Darwinbox highlights employee-group-based leave policies, flexible workflows, dashboards, mobile apply/approve, and payroll connection. ([SAP Learning][1])

---

# 1. Leave Management Main Structure

Your Leave module should have these main sections:

```text
Leave Management
├── Dashboard
├── My Leave
├── Team Leave
├── Leave Requests
├── Leave Calendar
├── Leave Balance
├── Leave Ledger
├── Leave Types
├── Leave Rules / Leave Policies
├── Leave Workflow
├── Leave Forms
├── Holiday Calendar
├── Weekly Off / Work Calendar
├── Leave Encashment
├── Leave Carry Forward
├── Comp-Off
├── Leave Adjustment
├── Bulk Actions
├── Imports
├── Reports
└── Settings
```

The important design is this:

```text
Leave Type = Type of leave
Example: Sick Leave, Casual Leave, Earned Leave

Leave Rule / Leave Policy = How that leave works
Example: 12 Sick Leaves yearly, monthly accrual, max 3 continuous days, document required after 2 days

Leave Workflow = Who approves it
Example: Reporting Manager → HR

Leave Form = What fields employee fills
Example: Date, reason, attachment, emergency contact

Leave Assignment = Which employees/entity get this policy
Example: Entity A employees only, or Grade M1-M3 only
```

# 4. Leave Rules / Leave Policy

Leave Rule is the real business logic. One leave rule can contain multiple leave types.

```text
Policy Name: India Permanent Employee Leave Policy 2026

Leave Types inside this rule:
- Casual Leave: 12 yearly, monthly accrual
- Sick Leave: 10 yearly, full credit at year start
- Earned Leave: 18 yearly, monthly accrual, carry forward allowed
- LOP: unlimited, unpaid
```

Zoho People supports customizable leave policies based on entitlement models such as fixed, experience, grant-based, or attendance-based entitlement for locations, roles, or departments. ([Zoho][4])

## 7.2 Leave Type inside Rule should have separate configuration

This is important.

The same leave type can behave differently in different rules.

Example:

```text
Sick Leave in India Policy = 10 days yearly
Sick Leave in UAE Policy = 15 days yearly
Sick Leave for Probation = 0 or 3 days
Sick Leave for Contract = unpaid only
```

So do not put all logic directly in Leave Type master. Put main behavior in **Leave Rule Leave Type Mapping**.

# 32. Example Complete Leave Rule

```text
Rule Name: India Permanent Employee Leave Policy 2026
Tenant: ABC Group
Entity: ABC India Pvt Ltd
Employee Type: Permanent
Leave Year: Apr-Mar
Effective From: 01-Apr-2026
Status: Active
```

### Leave types inside rule

```text
Casual Leave
- Entitlement: 12 days yearly
- Accrual: 1 day monthly
- Max continuous: 3 days
- Advance notice: 2 days
- Carry forward: No
- Encashment: No
- Clubbing: Not with Earned Leave
- Workflow: Reporting Manager

Sick Leave
- Entitlement: 10 days yearly
- Accrual: Full credit yearly
- Same day allowed: Yes
- Past date allowed: 2 days
- Medical certificate: Required if > 2 days
- Carry forward: No
- Workflow: Reporting Manager → HR if > 3 days

Earned Leave
- Entitlement: 18 days yearly
- Accrual: 1.5 days monthly
- Max continuous: 15 days
- Advance notice: 7 days
- Carry forward: Max 30 days
- Encashment: Allowed
- Workflow: Reporting Manager → HR

LOP
- Balance required: No
- Paid: No
- Payroll impact: Deduction
- Workflow: Reporting Manager → HR → Payroll
```

# 37. Final Architecture Logic

Use this mental model:

```text
Tenant
 └── Entity / Company
      └── Leave Rule / Policy
           ├── Leave Type: Casual Leave
           │    ├── Entitlement
           │    ├── Accrual
           │    ├── Restriction
           │    ├── Workflow
           │    └── Form
           ├── Leave Type: Sick Leave
           │    ├── Entitlement
           │    ├── Document Rule
           │    ├── Workflow
           │    └── Form
           └── Leave Type: Earned Leave
                ├── Carry Forward
                ├── Encashment
                ├── Workflow
                └── Payroll Mapping
```

This structure will let you support **default tenant policy**, **entity-wise custom policy**, **department-wise exception**, **employee-specific override**, and **fully editable leave application forms** without hardcoding.
